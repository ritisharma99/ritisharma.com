Trips & Exploration

Finding magic in the open road

I believe that the best way to understand the world is to move through it. Road trips have taught me more about geography, culture, human nature, and myself than any classroom ever could. There's something profoundly liberating about pointing a car in a direction and seeing where curiosity leads.

Every destination has a story — not just the major landmarks, but the small towns with forgotten diners, the scenic overlooks nobody talks about, the conversations with locals who share their history. I collect these moments more than I collect postcards. The goal isn't always to reach a destination; sometimes the journey is the point.

Why I Travel

  • Discovery
    Finding the Unexpected
    The best experiences happen off the beaten path — hidden trails, unexpected conversations, moments that weren't in any guidebook.
  • Connection
    Understanding People and Places
    Every region has its own rhythm, culture, and story. Traveling helps me understand how different communities think, create, and connect.
  • Reflection
    Thinking and Clarity
    Long drives give my mind space to wander, think deeply about problems, and return with fresh perspectives on work and life.

My favorite trips are the ones where I have flexibility — a rough direction, maybe a few interesting stops, but room to detour when something catches my eye. That's where the real adventure happens.
