About Me

I'm a bio-mechanical engineer and researcher driven by curiosity about how things break; and how they resist breaking. My path has taken me from building computational models of electrical waves in the heart to studying cellular energetics in diabetes, and now to uncovering how bone survives millions of cycles of stress. Across continents and disciplines, I've worked at the intersection of mechanics, imaging, and biology, always searching for the hidden principles that govern resilience in living systems.

Beyond the lab, I care deeply about building community, and creating environments where curiosity thrives. I love finding creative ways to explain science and make engineering feel intuitive and human. When I'm not working, I try to travel as much as I can; especially road trips where I get to drive for hours and explore new places. I also enjoy 3D printing, learning new tools just for fun, and reflecting on how scientific ideas connect to everyday life. I'm a realist with my feet on the ground, an optimist with my head in the clouds, and someone who believes that growth comes from staying endlessly curious.
